# Project Sunrise Premium Threshold Diagnostic

An independent analytical tool examining the fare premium required to support non-stop ultra-long-haul services on Qantas Project Sunrise routes.

The analysis does not ask whether the aircraft can operate the route. It asks a different commercial question:

**What premium must the market sustain for the non-stop service to cover the additional cost of reach?**

The tool compares Sydney–London and Sydney–New York under a range of fuel-price and operating scenarios to show how the required premium changes as route economics move.

---

## Analytical scope

The diagnostic examines:

- Sydney–London and Sydney–New York non-stop services
- Estimated operating economics
- Fuel-price sensitivity
- Load and revenue assumptions
- Cargo contribution
- Required fare premium
- Commercial threshold risk

The outputs are scenario-based estimates rather than predictions of actual Qantas fares, demand or route profitability.

---

## Data and assumptions

The analysis draws on publicly available aviation information, including airline, aircraft, route-distance and industry cost data, together with explicit modelling assumptions developed by the author.

Key assumptions include operating cost, fuel price, passenger load, cargo contribution and route-specific economics. These assumptions are used to estimate the additional fare premium required for non-stop service relative to the modelled baseline.

## Method

The diagnostic uses an independent route-economics model with sensitivity and simulation analysis.

The model tests how the required fare premium changes under different operating conditions, including:

- an anchor fuel-price scenario
- a higher fuel-price stress scenario
- changes in passenger load
- cargo contribution
- variation in route economics

The analysis is designed to identify the commercial threshold that must be cleared for non-stop ultra-long-haul service to remain economically supportable.

It does not establish whether passengers will pay that premium. That requires separate demand and willingness-to-pay evidence.

---

## Interpretation

The results should be read as a commercial diagnostic.

They indicate the approximate premium required under the stated assumptions, not:

- an official Qantas forecast
- a published Qantas cost estimate
- a prediction of future ticket prices
- a recommendation to operate or discontinue a route
- investment, financial or operational advice

The analysis is independent and is not commissioned, endorsed or produced by Qantas Airways, Airbus or any other airline, manufacturer or industry body.
